<p><p>Biological insecticides can be classified into three main types: microbial pesticide, plant pesticide, and biochemical pesticide. Microbial pesticides contain microorganisms that target specific insects. Plant pesticides are derived from plants and act as natural repellents or toxins against pests. Biochemical pesticides are made from naturally-occurring substances that interfere with the pests' biological processes. Each type of biological insecticide offers a unique approach to pest control, providing environmentally-friendly alternatives to traditional chemical pesticides.</p></p>
