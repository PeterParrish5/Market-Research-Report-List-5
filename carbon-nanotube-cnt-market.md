<p><p>Carbon Nanotubes (CNTs) are categorized into two main types - Single-Walled Carbon Nanotubes (SWCNTs) and Multi-Walled Carbon Nanotubes (MWCNTs). SWCNTs have unique properties such as high mechanical strength and electrical conductivity, making them suitable for applications in electronics, sensors, and nanotechnology. On the other hand, MWCNTs consist of multiple layers of graphene rolled into cylindrical structures, offering enhanced mechanical, thermal, and electrical properties. They are commonly used in aerospace, automotive, and energy storage applications.</p></p>
