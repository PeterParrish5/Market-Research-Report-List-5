<p><p>Switching amplifiers are categorized into digital amplifiers and analog amplifiers markets. Digital amplifiers use pulse-width modulation (PWM) techniques to amplify signals while analog amplifiers use continuous signals for amplification. Digital amplifiers offer advantages like higher efficiency, better control over output signals, and smaller size. Analog amplifiers, on the other hand, provide advantages in terms of smoother signal reproduction and simplicity of design. Both types of amplifiers cater to different market segments and offer unique benefits based on individual requirements.</p></p>
