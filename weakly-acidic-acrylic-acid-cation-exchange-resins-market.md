<p><ul><li>Type I</li><li>Type II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Weakly acidic acrylic acid cation exchange resins are commonly classified into two types: Type I and Type II. Type I resins have lower crosslinking density compared to Type II resins, resulting in a higher exchange capacity and faster kinetics. These resins are more suitable for applications requiring rapid ion exchange or high selectivity. On the other hand, Type II resins have a higher crosslinking density, providing better mechanical and chemical stability. They are often used in applications requiring prolonged use or harsh operating conditions.</p></p>
